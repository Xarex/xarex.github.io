The Edit Hours Project

February 1, 2019 — When it comes to custom work, it is not always easy to keep track of exactly how many hours you dedicated to a project, a task, or anything asked of you. Whether you begin the clock at a certain time, get tasked with other tasks or assignments at work, it can be easy to get distracted from what you are doing. With Edit Hours, you can track your time for doing specific tasks with notes to include to ensure you know exactly what you did, are doing, still have to do, etc.

This project was done for my primary company, MultiVu to help us keep track of the work we do for clients. Originally, it was going to be a paid web app, but it was made free for anyone to use. While the capabilities of keeping track of exact payment were not added in, this can easily be done by at least just knowing the time and multipying that time by the amount you should be getting paid. At most, up to 50 times will be shown at once. You can export the times from today up to a year ago, or even export every single time you have tracked. You can also view today only or a year ago, or every time you have tracked. If you are looking for something specific, you can search by title or by a note you left yourself. You also have the ability to sort times by ascending or descending times. That is really all there is to it. It is a basic time keeper to help you keep track of your projects.

The application is very easy to use and is as easy as hitting start, stop, and adding or editing the time.
